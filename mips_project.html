MIPS Simulator Project



About

In this project, I was part of a three person group tasked with creating the core part of a MIPS (Microprocessor without Interlocked Pipeline Stages)
processor simulator, called MySPIM. It was written in the C programming language and ran on a Unix platform. It simulated the functions of the MIPS processor,
such as the Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU), the fetching, partitioning, and decoding of instructions, reading and writing to memory, and many more.
Besides building my parts of the simulator, I also tested the project against the provided inputs and reported any problems that arose while testing
back to my teammates, and fixed any issues from my portion of the code.


What I Learned

One of the biggest things I learned from this project was team-work and leadership skills. This was my first group project in any technical class.
I learned how to schedule and streamline the coding of the simulator between three individuals.
I made sure everyone was up to date on what needed to be completed by the agreed upon timeline.
Additionally, it strenghtened my proficiency of the C language and helped to better understand MIPS, which we learned about in class.
